Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet every 5-7 years and immediately after any impact; look for visible damage, worn straps, or changes in fit as signs for replacement. Ensure it meets safety standards.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspect tires, brakes, and overall condition before riding your bike stored for a while. Ensure lights and components work properly.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a helmet, obey traffic laws, use hand signals, stay visible with lights and reflectors, ride with traffic, be predictable, watch for hazards, yield to pedestrians, stay alert, and maintain your bike regularly.
